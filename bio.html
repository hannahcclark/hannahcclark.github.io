About Me

I began programming my freshman year of high school, when I was required to take an introductory class by the engineering program I was part of. I greatly enjoyed it and proceed to take as many programming courses as the school offered, including an independent study in creating GUIs in Java and a Mobile App Development class focussed on iOS. As a capstone project for the program at the school, I developed a basic grade book software for OS X.

I am currently a junior at Tufts University School of Engineering majoring in Computer Science. Some of the more interesting courses I have taken include Concurrent Programming, in which the threading and message passing models of concurrency were explored using Python, C, and Erlang, and Operating Systems, focused on components of operating systems and tradeoffs in their design. I am currently working on a capstone project for my degree at Tufts, an iPad application for the radiology department at Tufts Medical to use in lectures for medical students and residents.

During my sophomore year, I was a teaching assistant for the Machine Structure and Assembly Language Programming course at Tufts. Since September 2014, I have been a teaching assistant for the Programming Languages course at Tufts, which has included leading a recitation class.

I also take part in the Computer Science Exchange and Women in Computer Science organizations at Tufts. I am a member of the Tau Beta Pi chapter at Tufts and am involved in its community service committee.

I enjoy hiking, rock climbing, and kayaking. My other hobbies include cooking, knitting, calligraphy, and indoor gardening.
