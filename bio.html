I began programming my freshman year of high school, and took as many programming courses as my high school offered, including an independent study in creating GUIs in Java, and a Mobile App Development class focussed on iOS. As a capstone project for the engineering program at the school, I developed a basic grade book software for OS X.

I'm currently a junior at Tufts University School of Engineering majoring in Computer Science. Some interesting courses I have taken include Concurrent Programming, in which the threading and message passing models of concurrency were explored using Python, C, and Erlang, and Operating Systems, focused on components of operating systems and tradeoffs in their design. I am currently working on a capstone project for my degree at Tufts, an iPad application for the radiology department at Tufts Medical to use in lectures for medical students and residents.

During my sophomore year, I was a teaching assistant for the Machine Structure and Assembly Language Programming course at Tufts. Since September 2014, I have been a teaching assistant for the Programming Languages course at Tufts.

I also take part in the Computer Science Exchange and Women in Computer Science organizations at Tufts. I am a member of the Tau Beta Pi chapter at Tufts and take part in its community service committee.

My other interests include cooking, knitting, calligraphy, hiking, rock climbing, and kayaking.
